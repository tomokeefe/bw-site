I started drawing letters on walls in 1989. I touched my first Mac in 1994. Since then the software has changed four times and the silicon six, and every new tool promised to replace the old one.

They didn't.

## What actually changed

The tools did. Pagemaker became QuarkXPress became InDesign. Photoshop 2.5 became Photoshop 26. Figma eaten by Adobe. AI image models arriving every six weeks. If you track your career by the software you know, you're chasing something that's re-inventing itself faster than you can master it.

## What didn't change

The eye. The ability to look at a composition and know whether the hierarchy is flat, the rhythm is broken, the type is fighting the photography, the grid is too polite. None of that is in the software. All of it is in the person.

## Why this matters for brands

A brand built by someone who only knows the tools will look exactly like whatever's in fashion the year it was made. Three years later it's stale. Five years later it's embarrassing.

A brand built by someone with the eye will look like a decision — not a default. Decisions age. Defaults decay.

That's the whole pitch.
